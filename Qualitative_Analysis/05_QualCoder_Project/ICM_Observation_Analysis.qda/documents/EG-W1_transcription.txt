F. Overall Impression and Summary Comments

Strengths observed during the session:
1. Students responded positively to collaborative drafting.
2. Coded feedback system was introduced clearly.
3. Teacher demonstrated good facilitation skills.
4. Most groups produced a collaborative draft.

Weaknesses / concerns observed:
1. Symbol confusion slowed down decoding phase.
2. Uneven participation within some groups.
3. Time management needs improvement.
4. Teacher occasionally provided direct answers instead of prompting.

General summary comment:
Week 1 of ICM implementation shows promise. Students are adapting to collaborative work and coded feedback. Frequent peer interaction is emerging, though not yet extensive. Initial challenges are typical for a new pedagogical approach; with continued practice, these issues are likely to resolve.

Recommendations for Subsequent Sessions:
1. Provide additional practice with symbol decoding before peer exchange.
2. Assign specific group roles to ensure equitable participation.
3. Encourage students to consult peers before approaching the teacher.
4. Reduce the number of coded symbols in early sessions.
5. Introduce structured peer exchange across groups.

Final Summary Comment from the Inspector:
The ICM has been introduced successfully. Students are engaged and willing to collaborate. Frequent peer interaction is already observable, though still developing. The coded feedback system is a significant shift from traditional correction, and students will need time to internalize it. The teacher should resist providing direct answers and instead prompt peer discussion.
