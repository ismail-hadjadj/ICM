F. Overall Impression and Summary Comments

Strengths observed during the session:
1. Teacher corrected 246 essays with consistency.
2. Model text was excellent.
3. Classroom management flawless.
4. Teacher maintained fidelity to traditional method.

Weaknesses / concerns observed:
1. No observable improvement in writing after six weeks.
2. Student engagement with feedback declined.
3. No revision ever required / no metalanguage development / no transfer.
4. Teacher effort did not translate to learning.

General summary comment:
Week 6 confirms what Week 1 suggested: traditional correction produces compliance, not competence. Students copy model texts beautifully but do not learn to correct their own errors. The same errors appear in Week 6 as in Week 1.

Recommendations for Subsequent Sessions:
1. Abandon passive review sessions; replace with structured revision.
2. Introduce peer discussion.
3. Teach students a simple coding system for self-correction.
4. Require revised drafts.

Final Summary Comment from the Inspector:
After six weeks, the traditional correction model produced no observable learning gains. The method is efficient for classroom management but ineffective for learning.
