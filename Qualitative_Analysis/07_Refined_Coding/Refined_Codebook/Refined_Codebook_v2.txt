Refined Codebook v2

Parent Code: Instructional_Format_and_Feedback
Child Code: Prompt_Activation
Definition: Teacher introduces the task, clarifies expectations, or activates prior knowledge.
Include: Prompt explanation, prior knowledge activation, task framing.
Exclude: Later guidance not focused on task setup.
Example Type of Evidence: Teacher spent 10 minutes introducing the prompt.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Helps establish implementation differences between conditions.

Parent Code: Instructional_Format_and_Feedback
Child Code: Coded_Feedback_Used
Definition: Feedback is provided through coded symbols rather than direct correction.
Include: Coded symbols, returned drafts with codes, coding system references.
Exclude: Direct written correction.
Example Type of Evidence: Students decoded symbols using reference cards.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: May explain stronger active processing in EG.

Parent Code: Instructional_Format_and_Feedback
Child Code: Direct_Correction_Used
Definition: Teacher provides direct written correction, underlining, or model answers.
Include: Red underlines, correct forms written, model text supplied.
Exclude: Indirect prompting only.
Example Type of Evidence: Essays returned with red underlines and correct forms.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: May explain passivity and weak transfer in CG.

Parent Code: Instructional_Format_and_Feedback
Child Code: Revision_Cycle_Completed
Definition: Lesson includes draft, feedback, revision, and closure via finalization or reflection.
Include: Final draft completed, presentation, reflection.
Exclude: Attempted but incomplete revision, aspirational recommendations.
Example Type of Evidence: All groups completed a final draft and presented.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: May explain consolidation of learning in EG.

Parent Code: Student_Engagement
Child Code: High_Engagement
Definition: Students actively attend to, process, or work with feedback.
Include: High ratings, active decoding/discussion/revision.
Exclude: Mere compliance without processing.
Example Type of Evidence: 92–95% actively engaged.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Supports interpretation of stronger EG outcomes.

Parent Code: Student_Engagement
Child Code: Low_or_Passive_Engagement
Definition: Students show minimal cognitive involvement or superficial attention.
Include: Brief glancing, passive listening, superficial copying.
Exclude: Mostly active engagement.
Example Type of Evidence: Students glanced briefly at corrections.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Helps explain weaker CG outcomes.

Parent Code: Student_Engagement
Child Code: On_Task_Behaviour
Definition: Students remain behaviorally focused on lesson activities.
Include: Orderly, quiet, sustained attention to tasks.
Exclude: Cognitive engagement claims by themselves.
Example Type of Evidence: Classroom orderly but passive.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Separates compliance from learning.

Parent Code: Peer_Interaction_and_Collaboration
Child Code: Within_Group_Discussion
Definition: Students discuss corrections or revisions within groups.
Include: Group debate, negotiation, collaborative drafting.
Exclude: Sitting together but working individually.
Example Type of Evidence: Groups debated corrections.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Explains collaborative learning processes in EG.

Parent Code: Peer_Interaction_and_Collaboration
Child Code: Across_Group_Exchange
Definition: Students exchange drafts or comments across groups.
Include: Inter-group draft exchange, presentations, peer comments.
Exclude: Within-group work only.
Example Type of Evidence: Groups exchanged drafts and gave comments.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Explains broader peer-mediated learning in EG.

Parent Code: Peer_Interaction_and_Collaboration
Child Code: No_Peer_Interaction
Definition: Peer discussion or collaboration is absent.
Include: Silent individual work, no discussion encouraged.
Exclude: Limited but real interaction.
Example Type of Evidence: No peer interaction across six weeks.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Explains isolated correction in CG.

Parent Code: Peer_Interaction_and_Collaboration
Child Code: Uneven_Participation
Definition: Collaboration exists but participation is not equally distributed.
Include: Dominant speakers, quieter students passive.
Exclude: Equitable collaboration.
Example Type of Evidence: Some groups had dominant speakers.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Useful for early implementation challenges in EG.

Parent Code: Metalinguistic_Use_and_Revision
Child Code: Metalinguistic_Use_Present
Definition: Students use grammatical or linguistic terminology to discuss errors.
Include: Terms like tense, agreement, article, spelling.
Exclude: Implicit correction without terminology.
Example Type of Evidence: Students used tense and agreement.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: May explain deeper error processing in EG.

Parent Code: Metalinguistic_Use_and_Revision
Child Code: Metalinguistic_Use_Absent
Definition: Students do not use grammatical terminology or cannot name error categories.
Include: No metalanguage observed, cannot name error types.
Exclude: Even limited terminology present.
Example Type of Evidence: Students could not name error types.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Helps explain shallow processing in CG.

Parent Code: Metalinguistic_Use_and_Revision
Child Code: Student_Initiated_Revision
Definition: Students revise or correct errors independently or with peers.
Include: Self-correction, peer-supported correction, independent revision.
Exclude: Teacher supplies correction directly.
Example Type of Evidence: Students corrected errors without prompting.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Likely linked to improved EG writing performance.

Parent Code: Metalinguistic_Use_and_Revision
Child Code: No_Revision_Transfer
Definition: Students do not carry feedback into later writing or revision.
Include: No revision attempted, copying only, same errors persist.
Exclude: Partial but real revision.
Example Type of Evidence: No revision attempted across six weeks.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Explains limited CG improvement.

Parent Code: Teacher_Role
Child Code: Teacher_as_Facilitator
Definition: Teacher prompts, monitors, or guides without supplying answers directly.
Include: Prompting, monitoring, redirecting to peers.
Exclude: Direct correction or answer giving.
Example Type of Evidence: Teacher asked: What do your group members think?
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Supports learner autonomy and collaborative revision.

Parent Code: Teacher_Role
Child Code: Teacher_as_Corrector
Definition: Teacher mainly provides correction, answers, or model text.
Include: Fixes errors, gives correct version, model provider.
Exclude: Indirect dialogic guidance.
Example Type of Evidence: Teacher corrected all essays and provided model text.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: May reinforce dependence in CG.

Parent Code: Teacher_Role
Child Code: Teacher_Prompting_without_Answer
Definition: Teacher intervenes through prompting rather than direct correction.
Include: Redirects students to peers, asks guiding questions.
Exclude: Gives answer anyway.
Example Type of Evidence: Students asked peers first, then teacher.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Indicates mature facilitation in EG.

Parent Code: Teacher_Role
Child Code: Teacher_Confirmation_Dependency
Definition: Students rely on teacher validation before applying corrections.
Include: Is this correct? repeated teacher validation-seeking.
Exclude: Resolving issues mainly within groups.
Example Type of Evidence: Students frequently asked teacher for confirmation.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Useful marker of limited autonomy.

Parent Code: Constraints_and_Limitations
Child Code: Symbol_Confusion
Definition: Students struggle to interpret coded feedback symbols.
Include: Confusion with symbol meanings, reliance on cards.
Exclude: Symbols fully internalized.
Example Type of Evidence: One-third struggled to recall symbol meanings.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Marks early EG implementation challenge.

Parent Code: Constraints_and_Limitations
Child Code: Time_Management_Problem
Definition: Time is used inefficiently or phases do not progress smoothly.
Include: Delayed transitions, revision took longer than planned.
Exclude: Smooth timing and completed phases.
Example Type of Evidence: Decoding and revision took longer than planned.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Shows early implementation strain or unproductive time use.

Parent Code: Constraints_and_Limitations
Child Code: Mechanical_Copying
Definition: Students copy text or correction without analysis or understanding.
Include: Copying model text verbatim, superficial completion.
Exclude: Original revision or adaptation.
Example Type of Evidence: Students copied model text without analysis.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Explains lack of transfer in CG.

Parent Code: Constraints_and_Limitations
Child Code: Lack_of_Autonomy
Definition: Students depend heavily on teacher correction or do not self-correct.
Include: Waiting for teacher, no self-correction culture.
Exclude: Independent revision.
Example Type of Evidence: Students waited for teacher to correct errors.
Likely Relevance to Quantitative Findings: Helps explain limited CG development and early EG reliance.
