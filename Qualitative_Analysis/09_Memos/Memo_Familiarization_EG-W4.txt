Document ID: EG-W4
Group: Experimental (ICM)
Week: W4

1. Overall snapshot
By Week 4, the ICM appears to have moved beyond initial implementation into routine operation. Students now seem to understand the coded system well, interact more extensively, and manage the revision cycle with much greater autonomy and confidence.

2. Most visible classroom processes
- Collaborative drafting was immediate and more structured.
- Coded feedback was decoded fluently with frequent use of reference cards.
- Group discussion and debate over corrections were active and sustained.
- Cross-group exchange and presentation were both visible.
- Final revision and reflection were completed within the session.

3. Student engagement pattern
Engagement was very high, estimated at about 90–95%. Students remained on task, transitions were smooth, and off-task behaviour was absent.

4. Peer interaction pattern
Peer interaction was extensive both within and between groups. Students discussed corrections confidently and sought peers before the teacher.

5. Teacher role pattern
The teacher’s role had shifted further toward prompting and facilitation. Instead of supplying answers, the teacher encouraged students to consult one another and think through corrections collaboratively.

6. Evidence of revision / metalinguistic use / autonomy
There is strong evidence of all three. Students used metalinguistic terms confidently, identified and corrected errors without teacher prompting, and completed revised drafts regularly.

7. Issues or constraints noted
Only minor constraints remained, including a small minority of less engaged students and a need for targeted support or extension activities once some groups finished early.

8. Relevance for later coding
This document is likely to be especially important for codes related to routinized implementation, extensive peer interaction, metalinguistic confidence, autonomous revision, high engagement, and teacher-as-facilitator.
