Document ID: CG-W6
Group: Control (Traditional)
Week: W6

1. Overall snapshot
The final observation of the control group shows continuity rather than development. The traditional correction model remained orderly and administratively consistent, but it did not generate student engagement, revision, peer learning, or visible improvement in writing.

2. Most visible classroom processes
- Teacher repeated the same prompt-explanation and individual drafting pattern.
- Drafts continued to be corrected directly by the teacher.
- Students reviewed corrections only superficially.
- A model text was again provided and copied.
- No peer discussion, revision cycle, or reflective activity was integrated.

3. Student engagement pattern
Engagement with correction had declined further, estimated at about 10–15%. Students appeared increasingly passive and cognitively disengaged.

4. Peer interaction pattern
Peer interaction remained absent across the six-week period. Students sat in the same physical arrangement but did not function collaboratively.

5. Teacher role pattern
The teacher remained a corrector and model provider. Although the teacher’s work was consistent and accurate, students learned to wait for correction rather than participate in the correction process.

6. Evidence of revision / metalinguistic use / autonomy
There was no visible revision, no metalinguistic development, and no student autonomy. Students copied model texts but did not transfer corrections to their own writing.

7. Issues or constraints noted
The main issues were declining engagement, entrenched passivity, over-reliance on teacher correction, absence of peer interaction, no transfer, and lack of observable learning gains.

8. Relevance for later coding
This document is important for codes related to entrenched passivity, declining engagement, no peer interaction, no revision, no metalinguistic use, direct correction, and absence of learning transfer.
