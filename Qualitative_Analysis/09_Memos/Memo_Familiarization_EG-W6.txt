Document ID: EG-W6
Group: Experimental (ICM)
Week: W6

1. Overall snapshot
Week 6 shows a mature and stabilized ICM classroom. The method is no longer merely being implemented; it has been internalized by students, who now operate with high autonomy, strong peer collaboration, consistent metalinguistic use, and minimal teacher intervention.

2. Most visible classroom processes
- Groups formed automatically and role-sharing was well established.
- Students decoded coded feedback fluently without needing reference support.
- Peer discussion was sophisticated and students justified alternatives.
- Draft revision, presentation, and reflection were completed smoothly.
- The entire feedback cycle was completed within the allocated time.

3. Student engagement pattern
Engagement remained very high, estimated at about 92–95%. Students were fully focused and the classroom showed sustained productive participation.

4. Peer interaction pattern
Peer interaction was extensive, constructive, and productive. Students resolved most issues within groups, exchanged drafts, and provided substantive peer feedback.

5. Teacher role pattern
The teacher functioned primarily as observer and facilitator. Intervention was minimal because students managed most meaning-making and correction processes themselves.

6. Evidence of revision / metalinguistic use / autonomy
Evidence here is very strong. Students named error types confidently, initiated corrections independently, revised regularly, and showed clear transfer to improved writing quality.

7. Issues or constraints noted
Only a very small minority of lower-engagement students remained. Otherwise, the earlier Week 1 challenges appear largely resolved.

8. Relevance for later coding
This document will be central for codes related to full implementation fidelity, autonomous revision, productive peer discourse, metalinguistic mastery, high engagement, transfer, and minimal teacher intervention.
